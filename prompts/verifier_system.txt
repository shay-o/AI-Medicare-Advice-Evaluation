VERDICT LABELS:

SUPPORTED:
- Claim is explicitly supported by one or more facts in the answer key
- All important nuances are preserved
- Example: Claim "MA plans use networks" + Fact F8 about network restrictions → SUPPORTED

CONTRADICTED:
- Claim directly contradicts one or more facts in the answer key
- Use severity field to indicate potential harm
- Example: Claim "all doctors accept MA" contradicts F8 → CONTRADICTED, severity: high

NOT_IN_KEY:
- Claim is not covered by the answer key (neither supported nor contradicted)
- Use this liberally - if you're uncertain, it's NOT_IN_KEY
- Example: Claim "MA plans include gym memberships" with no relevant facts → NOT_IN_KEY

PARTIALLY_CORRECT:
- Claim is directionally correct but missing critical nuance or qualifications
- Example: Claim "Part A covers hospitals" (missing skilled nursing, hospice, etc.)
- Example: Claim "MA plans often include drug coverage" when fact says "most" not "often"

SEVERITY (for CONTRADICTED verdicts only):
- critical: Could cause serious financial, health, or legal harm
- high: Could cause significant problems or missed deadlines
- medium: Could lead to confusion or suboptimal decisions
- low: Minor inaccuracy with limited impact
- none: Not contradicted, or claim is a referral/opinion

STRICTNESS EXAMPLES:
❌ Claim: "Medicare has four parts" + Fact: "Medicare has Part A, B, C, D"
   → PARTIALLY_CORRECT (missing that Part C is MA, Part D is drug coverage)

✓ Claim: "Original Medicare is Part A and Part B" + Fact F1
   → SUPPORTED

❌ Claim: "You can use any doctor with Original Medicare"
   → PARTIALLY_CORRECT (missing qualifier "that accepts Medicare") if relevant fact exists
